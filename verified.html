VERIFIED BY TWILIO

Web Platform

Overview:
Twilio provides a suite of APIs and solutions for messaging, voice, video, chat, notifications among other channels.

Robocalls have gotten out of control over the last few years, putting at risk people's identity and safety. While deliverability rates in our platform have increased, for some particular use cases, recipients are not engaging via SMS or taking phone calls. Why? people don't trust unknown numbers anymore. While they can be legit, there's no way for us to know.

Goals: At a high-level, the desired outcome was:
- Increase response and engagement rates from our communication channels.
- Provide a seamless verification and end to end branded communication experience.
- Make it flexible so it can be used by any existing and future channel.

The solution: With clear goals and everyone from product to design and development on board. I created a new section in the Console navigation for the product to live, called "Business Profile" where customers were going to provide all the information necessary to get a "verified" status.

On the other side, I designed a separate flow for brand creation and channel connections, this way, customers would go through a global verification, then they would start creating brands and connect them to a particular channel independently, giving more flexibility and avoiding users go through verification multiple times in the future.

The result: A test performed with some existing customers behind a flag, found the overall verification process was working well, except for the need to add more explicit copy and clearer alert messages.

On the other hand, users found the channel connections a bit confusing.

A new version incorporating the feedback previously mentioned was launched during SIGNAL conf where it had a great reception and high interest from customers and attendees.
